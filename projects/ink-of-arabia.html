Summary

Ink of Arabia is an editorial web piece shaped around calligraphy, ritual, and the feeling of ink drying in warm light.

Concept

The design treats letterforms as architecture: spacious, reverent, and grounded in a museum-like reading pace.

Design / Build

The system favors large typography, careful captions, responsive story blocks, and placeholder-safe image areas for future manuscript and process photography.

Reflection / Outcome

The project pushed the archive language toward cultural storytelling while keeping the tone respectful, slow, and image-led.
